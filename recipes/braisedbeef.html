Braised beef

Home

Description

A traditional Italian recipe using hanger steak, served with black truffle mashed potatoes.

Prep Time

  • Prep time: 35 minutes
  • Cook Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
  • Servings: 4
  • Total Time: 2 hours 20 minutes to 4 hours 20 minutes

Ingredients

  • Beef: Hanger steak, 2.2 lbs (1 kg), cut into 1.5–2 inch pieces
  • Vegetables: 2 white onions, 2 carrots, 2 celery ribs, 2 garlic cloves
  • Herbs & Seasonings: 3 sprigs rosemary, salt and black pepper to taste, 1 spoonful tomato paste
  • Liquids: 2.1 cups (500 ml) full-bodied red wine (e.g., Chianti), 2.1 cups (500 ml) meat broth
  • Oils & Fats: Extra virgin olive oil to taste
  • For Mashed Potatoes: 1.8 lbs (800 g) waxy potatoes, 0.4 cup (100 ml) milk, 1.75 oz (50 g) butter, salt and pepper to taste

Step by Step

  1. Vegetable oil – Heat 2 tbsp in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  2. Beef chuck roast (5 lbs) – Cut into 3–4 inch chunks, season with salt and pepper, then sear in batches until deeply browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Fennel, onion, carrots – Sauté in the beef drippings over medium-low heat for 5 minutes until softened.
  4. Garlic – Add and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Tomato paste (2 tbsp) – Stir in and cook for 1 minute until darkened and fragrant.
  6. Red wine (2½ cups) – Pour in to deglaze, scraping up browned bits from the pot bottom.
  7. Beef broth (1 cup) – Add with the wine or after, ensuring liquid comes halfway up the beef.
  8. Mushrooms – Stir into the liquid.
  9. Bay leaves, thyme sprigs – Add for aroma.
  10. Browned beef – Return to the pot in a single layer, nestling into the liquid (not submerged).
  11. Accumulated juices – Pour over the meat.

Cooking Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. After searing beef and sautéing vegetables, deglaze with wine and broth.
  3. Return beef to the pot, add herbs, and bring to a simmer.
  4. Cover and transfer to the oven. Braise for 2.5 to 3 hours, until fork-tender.
  5. Optional: Flip beef halfway through
  6. Remove beef, rest covered with foil. Strain and reduce sauce if desired.
  7. Serve beef with vegetables and thickened sauce
